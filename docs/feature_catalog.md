# Feature Catalog

## Purpose

This document describes the main feature groups used in the Football Risk Analytics project, along with their role, intuition, and operational relevance.

The project is not intended as a medical injury prediction system. Instead, it builds a **risk proxy** for elevated short-term workload exposure. Feature design is therefore centered on:

- workload accumulation
- workload concentration
- form and short-term trend context
- interpretability
- temporal robustness

---

## Dataset Context

The main integrated feature table is:

- `player_dataset_final`

The predictive modeling dataset is:

- `player_dataset_predictive`

The predictive dataset adds a forward-shifted target:
- `high_risk_next`

---

## Feature Design Principles

### 1. Time-aware construction
All features are intended to use information available at or before the current player-match observation.

### 2. Operational interpretability
Features were selected to remain understandable for performance and medical stakeholders.

### 3. Workload-first philosophy
The strongest emphasis is on workload exposure rather than direct physiological or medical injury labels.

### 4. Multi-scale information
The feature set combines:
- immediate load
- medium horizon load
- recent match sequence effects
- short-term performance form

## Feature Groups

## A. Entity and Context Columns

These columns are used for identification, joins, filtering, or operational outputs rather than as predictive inputs.

| Column | Type | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `player_id` | identifier | unique player key |
| `competition_id` | identifier | competition context |
| `season_id` | identifier | season context |
| `match_id` | identifier | unique match key |
| `match_date` | date | chronological ordering |
| `team` | categorical/string | team context |

These are generally retained in outputs but are not the main numeric model features.

## B. Match-Level Per-90 Performance Features

These originate from match-level event aggregation combined with player minute information.

### 1. `shots_per90`
**Definition**  
Estimated shots produced by the player, normalized to 90 minutes.

**Interpretation**  
Higher values indicate greater shooting involvement.

**Why it matters**  
A more involved player may simultaneously carry greater physical demand, tactical responsibility, or sustained exposure.

---

### 2. `xg_per90`
**Definition**  
Expected goals generated by the player per 90 minutes.

**Interpretation**  
Proxy for attacking involvement and high-value offensive actions.

**Why it matters**  
Serves as a signal of short-term offensive load and usage intensity.

---

### 3. `passes_per90`
**Definition**  
Pass count normalized to 90 minutes.

**Interpretation**  
Represents ball circulation participation and tactical centrality.

**Why it matters**  
Helps contextualize involvement profile, especially across different player roles.

---

### 4. `carries_per90`
**Definition**  
Carry count normalized to 90 minutes.

**Interpretation**  
Signals ball progression involvement and movement burden.

**Why it matters**  
Can correlate with repeated movement intensity and role-specific load.

---

### 5. `progressive_x_per90`
**Definition**  
Approximate progressive ball advancement per 90 minutes.

**Interpretation**  
A proxy for progressive contribution and dynamic possession advancement.

**Why it matters**  
Adds a more movement-oriented performance signal beyond raw event counts.

---

## C. Short-Term Form Features

These capture recent match-based form and trends.

### 6. `xg_last_5`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of expected goals over the player’s last 5 matches.

**Interpretation**  
Short-term attacking output trend.

**Why it matters**  
Provides context for recent usage and form intensity.

---

### 7. `shots_last_5`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of shots over the player’s last 5 matches.

**Interpretation**  
Short-term shot involvement.

**Why it matters**  
Works alongside `xg_last_5` to capture recent offensive volume.

---

### 8. `progressive_last_5`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of progressive contribution over the player’s last 5 matches.

**Interpretation**  
Recent progression and dynamic involvement.

**Why it matters**  
Adds form information beyond finishing events.

---

### 9. `trend_xg_3v3`
**Definition**  
Difference between xG in the most recent 3 matches and the prior 3-match window.

**Interpretation**  
A direction-of-form measure rather than a static recent average.

**Why it matters**  
Captures acceleration or deceleration in recent offensive involvement.

---

## D. Rolling Workload Features

These are central to the project and are designed from player minute exposure.

### 10. `minutes_last_7d`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of minutes played over the last 7 days.

**Interpretation**  
Acute workload.

**Why it matters**  
One of the most important immediate workload measures.

---

### 11. `minutes_last_14d`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of minutes played over the last 14 days.

**Interpretation**  
Intermediate workload horizon.

**Why it matters**  
Helps situate acute load within a broader recent context.

---

### 12. `minutes_last_28d`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of minutes played over the last 28 days.

**Interpretation**  
Chronic workload proxy.

**Why it matters**  
Provides medium-term accumulated exposure.

---

### 13. `minutes_last_5_matches`
**Definition**  
Rolling sum of minutes played over the last 5 matches.

**Interpretation**  
Match-sequence-based load rather than calendar-time load.

**Why it matters**  
Adds robustness where fixture congestion is uneven.

---

## E. Workload Ratio Feature

### 14. `acwr`
**Definition**  
Acute-to-chronic workload ratio. In the selected `true` implementation:

`minutes_last_7d / (minutes_last_28d / 4.0)`

subject to a validity condition such as minimum recent chronic minutes.

**Interpretation**  
Measures how elevated acute load is relative to recent baseline load.

**Why it matters**  
This is a core risk proxy in the project and is central to the target definition.

**Caution**  
ACWR should be interpreted carefully. It is useful as a governance-oriented workload signal, but it is not a direct medical injury probability.

---

## Target Variables

## 1. `high_risk`
**Definition**  
Binary proxy label defined in `player_dataset_final` from the ACWR threshold.

Example logic:
- `1` if `acwr > 1.5`
- `0` otherwise

**Role**  
Current-period workload risk proxy.

---

## 2. `high_risk_next`
**Definition**  
Forward-shifted target constructed in `player_dataset_predictive` using `LEAD(high_risk)` within each player-season ordered by match date.

**Role**  
Training target for predictive modeling.

**Why it matters**  
Allows the model to forecast elevated next-observation workload risk rather than simply describe the current state.

## Missingness Considerations

Several features can legitimately contain missing values at certain points in the pipeline.

### Typical causes
- insufficient lookback history
- low chronic workload causing ACWR invalidation
- missing event-derived history
- left joins preserving row coverage

### Practical implications
The inference pipeline currently handles missing values pragmatically, but the recommended future state is a full sklearn preprocessing pipeline with:

- `SimpleImputer`
- `StandardScaler`
- `LogisticRegression`

This will ensure feature handling is consistent between training and inference.

## Role of Features in Governance

These features were chosen not only for predictive utility but also for governance value.

### Interpretability
The feature set is understandable to technical football stakeholders.

### Temporal robustness
Workload windows and recent-form features are suited to non-stationary sports environments.

### Operational actionability
The model output can be linked back to familiar workload concepts:
- acute minutes
- chronic minutes
- recent match burden
- ratio-based overload

This makes the system more credible for decision support than a fully opaque feature space.

---

## Feature Caveats

### Role heterogeneity
The same feature may have different implications by player role.

### Data limitation
No GPS, biomechanics, internal load, wellness, or clinical data are included.

### Proxy target dependency
Because `high_risk` is derived from ACWR, the system should be interpreted as a workload-governance model, not as an injury diagnosis engine.

### Structural non-stationarity
Football workloads vary substantially across competition phases, rotations, and fixture congestion. Features should always be interpreted in that context.

## Summary

The current feature catalog is intentionally compact, interpretable, and workload-centered. It captures:

- player involvement
- recent form
- acute and chronic workload
- workload ratio stress
- forward risk labeling

That combination makes the feature set suitable for a governance-oriented football risk analytics system where interpretability and operational usefulness are more important than maximizing black-box performance.
